SIMULATIONS with different initial conditions

 

Simulation #1 The pendulum is given a push to start its motion

Initial conditions

 

 

The plots show that there are two equilibrium positions for the pendulum. These two equilibrium points are called fixed points in chaotic dynamics. The two fixed points are:

1.    When the pendulum is hanging down from its pivot point . This is a stable equilibrium point.

2.    When the pendulum is standing directly above the pivot point. This is an unstable equilibrium point.

 

The push to start the motion was such that the kinetic energy imparted to the pendulum causes it to reach its highest point directly above the pivot point with approximately zero kinetic energy. This point is an unstable equilibrium point and the pendulum hovers at this point until it swings downwards.

 

This system is called a non-linear dynamical system since the differential equation describing the system contains a non-linear term. The graph of the restoring force against the angular displacement is not linear but of the form . The system oscillates in a periodic manner. The phase plot is forms a closed (complete loop) the orbit in phase space is periodic. The total energy of the system is conserved because we have assumed that there are no dissipative forces acting.

 

Simulation #2 The pendulum falls from rest

Initial conditions

 

The initial angle for the angular displacement is 30o. For small angular displacements (< ~30o), the force is approximately a linear function of the angular displacement. Hence, the motion of the pendulum bob is approximately simple harmonic motion with period T.

 

 

The potential energy curve is a parabolic function. The potential is the potential energy divided by the mass of the system. So, the potential is a quadratic function .

 

The phase space diagram for simple harmonic motion is an ellipse.
