SOLVING THE [1D] SCHRODINGER EQUATION

WITH THE FINITE DIFFERENCE TIME DOMAIN METHOD

Motion of an electron under the influence of a constant electric field

 

              view documentation

 

 

The [1D] Schrodinger Equation is solved with the finite difference time development method to create the animations shown below.

 

An electron is modelled as a wave packet. The wave packet is a sinusoidal function with a Gaussian envelope. The electron can be though as a particle and the classical laws of physics can be used to predict the time evolution of expectation values. But the wave nature of the electron means that we do not know the precise values of position and velocity at an instant. We can’t predict the path of an electron, we can only predict the probability of finding the electron at each instant within a range of x values.

 

The animations below show the motion of our wave packet for (1) a zero applied electric field; (2) an accelerating electric field and (3) a retarding electric field.
